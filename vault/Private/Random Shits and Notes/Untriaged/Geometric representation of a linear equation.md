### **Geometric Interpretation of a Linear Equation**  

1. **Understanding Variables and Geometry**  
   - A **linear equation in two variables** ($x_1, x_2$) represents a **line** in a 2D plane.  
   - A **linear equation in three variables** ($x_1, x_2, x_3$) represents a **plane** in 3D space.  

1. **Two-Variable Case ($x_1, x_2$) – Lines in 2D**  
   - Each equation represents a **line**.  
   - The **solution** is where the lines **intersect**
   ![[Pasted image 20250323142707.png]]

   **Possible Cases:**  
   ![[Pasted image 20250323144126.png]]
   - **Unique solution** → The lines **intersect at one point**.  
   - **No solution** → The lines are **parallel and never meet**.  
   - **Infinite solutions** → The lines **completely overlap**.  

3. **Three-Variable Case ($x_1, x_2, x_3$) – Planes in 3D**  
   - Each equation represents a **plane**.  
   - The **solution** is where the planes **intersect**.  
![[Pasted image 20250323145135.png]]
   **Possible Cases:**  
   - **Unique solution** → The three planes **intersect at a single point**.  
   - **No solution** → The planes are **parallel or inconsistent** (do not meet).  
   - **Infinite solutions** → The planes **intersect along a line or completely overlap**.
