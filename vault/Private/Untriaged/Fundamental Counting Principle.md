### **2. The Two Fundamental Rules**

- **Multiplication Rule (AND):** If you have m ways to do task A **AND** then n ways to do task B, total ways = m×n.
    
    - _Example:_ 3 shirts, 2 pants. Total outfits = 3×2=6.
        
- **Addition Rule (OR):** If you can do task A in $m$ ways **OR** task B in $n$ ways (and you can't do both), total ways = $m+n$ .
    
    - _Example:_ You can fly (3 airlines) OR take a train (2 lines). Total options = 3+2=5.

### The **Fundamental Counting Principle** states that:

> If there are $n$ independent choices, and each choice can be made in $m_1, m_2, \dots, m_n$ ways, then the total number of outcomes is:
$$
m_1 \times m_2 \times \cdots \times m_n
$$

---

### **EXAMPLE**
- There are **3 walls**, each of which can be painted in **2 colors** (black or white).
- Since each wall's color choice is independent of the others, the total number of ways to color the walls is:
$$
2 \times 2 \times 2 = 2^3 = 8
$$

This is a direct application of the **Fundamental Counting Principle**. It ensures we systematically count all possible combinations without missing any or double-counting.

---

### **Why It Works Here**
Each "choice" (color for a wall) is independent:
- The first wall can be either **black** or **white**.
- The second wall can also independently be **black** or **white**.
- The third wall is similarly independent.

So, the principle combines all these possibilities into $2 \times 2 \times 2$.
