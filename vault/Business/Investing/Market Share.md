
## 💼 What Does *Market Share* Mean?

**Market share** = The **percentage of total sales in a market** that one company holds.

### For Coca-Cola, this means:
> “Out of every ₹100 (or $100) spent on soft drinks globally, how much is spent on Coca-Cola products?”

So if Coke has 50% market share, it means:
- Out of all soft drinks sold, **50% are Coca-Cola products** (Coke, Sprite, Fanta, etc.)

It’s **not** about:
- The company’s total valuation (that’s market cap)
- The brand value in isolation (though it helps)
- How many people *like* it — it’s about how many *buy* it

---

## 🔍 Example:

Let’s say globally, soft drink sales = $1 trillion/year  
If Coca-Cola sells $500 billion worth → it has **50% market share**

Same goes for regional:
- In India, if ₹100 crore worth of sodas are sold
- And ₹40 crore comes from Coca-Cola → it has **40% market share** in India

---

## 🧠 So What Did Buffett Mean?

> “If you gave me $100 billion, I couldn’t take Coca-Cola’s market share.”

He meant:
- Even with insane money, it’d be **nearly impossible** to make people **stop drinking Coke** and switch to something else
- Coca-Cola’s **distribution, pricing, loyalty, and brand** are *so strong* that its market share is hard to steal
- That’s what makes it a “forever business”

---

## 💎 In Summary:

| Term            | Means                                                                 |
|-----------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Market share**| % of total sales in the industry                                      |
| **Valuation**   | What the stock market thinks the company is worth                     |
| **Brand value** | The intangible value of how strong the brand is (trust, love, etc.)   |
| **Moat**        | All the strengths that protect Coca-Cola’s market share (brand, dist) |
