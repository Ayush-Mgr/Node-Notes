## **What is an Equation?**

An **equation** is a mathematical statement that asserts the equality of two expressions. It consists of:

- **Variables** (unknowns, like $x,y,z$)
    
- **Coefficients** (numbers multiplying the variables)
    
- **Constants** (fixed values)
    
- **An equal sign ($=$)**

### **Example of a General Equation:**

$$2x+3=7$$

Here:

- $x$ is the **variable** (unknown we need to find).
    
- 2 is the **coefficient** of $x$.
    
- 3 and 7 are **constants**.
    

Solving it:
$$2x+3=7$$
$$2x=7−3$$
$$2x=4$$
$$x=
\frac{4}{2}$$
$$x= 2$$


Thus, $x=2$ is the **solution**.


## **🔹 General Rule:**

- If you have **1 variable** → You need **1 equation** to solve for it.
    
- If you have **2 variables** ($x_1,x_2$​) → You need **2 independent equations** to solve for both.
    
- If you have **3 variables** ($x_1,x_2,x_3$​) → You need **3 independent equations** for a unique solution.
    
- If you have **n variables** ($x_1​,x_2​,...,x_n$​) → You need **n independent equations** to find a unique solution.
    

---

## **🔹 Geometric Interpretation**

- **1 variable (1D)** → A **point** on a number line.
    
- **2 variables (2D)** → Two **lines** in a plane (intersection gives a point).
    
- **3 variables (3D)** → Three **planes** in space (intersection gives a point).
    
- **n variables (n-D)** → n-dimensional hyperplanes (intersection gives a single solution in n-space).
    

If you have **fewer equations than variables**, you will get **infinite solutions** or **no solution** (underdetermined system).

If you have **more equations than variables**, the system might be **overdetermined** (sometimes inconsistent, meaning no solution).
