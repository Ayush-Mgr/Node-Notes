Used when you are comparing the **means of two different groups**.


## 🧠 What is it?

A **two-sample t-test** compares the **means of two independent groups** to determine if there's a statistically significant difference between them.

---

## ✅ When to Use?

Use this test if:

- You’re comparing **two independent groups** (e.g., males vs. females).
- **Population variances** are **unknown but assumed equal**.
- Data is **normally distributed**.

---

## 🔍 Key Concepts

- Use **Z-test** when **population standard deviations** are known.
- Use **t-test** when **sample standard deviations** are used.
- Use **independent t-test** for comparing two **different groups**.
- Use **paired t-test** for **before-after** or matched sample comparisons.

---

## 📐 Test Statistic (Equal Variances Assumed)

$$
t = \frac{\bar{X}_1 - \bar{X}_2}{\sqrt{\frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_2^2}{n_2}}}
$$

Where:

- $\bar{X}_1, \bar{X}_2$ = sample means  
- $s_1, s_2$ = sample standard deviations  
- $n_1, n_2$ = sample sizes

This is known as the **Welch’s t-test**, used when variances are **not assumed equal**.


---

## 📊 Example

> A researcher wants to know if there is a difference in weekly study hours between males and females.

Assume the following:

- Males: $\bar{X}_1 = 12$, $s_1 = 2$, $n_1 = 30$  
- Females: $\bar{X}_2 = 14$, $s_2 = 2.5$, $n_2 = 30$

---

### Step 1: Calculate the t-statistic

$$
t = \frac{12 - 14}{\sqrt{\frac{2^2}{30} + \frac{2.5^2}{30}}}$$
$$= \frac{-2}{\sqrt{\frac{4}{30} + \frac{6.25}{30}}}$$
$$= \frac{-2}{\sqrt{0.1333 + 0.2083}}$$
$$= \frac{-2}{\sqrt{0.3416}} \approx \frac{-2}{0.5844} \approx -3.42
$$

---

### Step 2: Degrees of Freedom (optional)

Use the Welch–Satterthwaite formula to compute approximate $df$, or use software.

---

### Step 3: Conclusion

Compare with a critical value for the given degrees of freedom and significance level (e.g., $\alpha = 0.05$).

Since $|t| = 3.42$ is likely greater than the critical value, **reject $H_0$**.  
There is significant evidence that study hours differ between groups.
