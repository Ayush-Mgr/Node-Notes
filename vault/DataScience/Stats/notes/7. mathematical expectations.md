## variance of  X
### **Why Do We Need the Variance?**

The variance of a random variable $X$, denoted as $\text{Var}(X)$, is a measure of how much the values of $X$ deviate from their mean (expected value). Here's why it's important:

1. **Measures Uncertainty**: While the expected value gives us the "center" of the distribution, the variance tells us how spread out the values are around this center. 
   - A high variance means outcomes vary widely.
   - A low variance indicates outcomes are closer to the expected value.

2. **reliability:** helps us measure the reliability of the expected value , 
   - high Variance low readability low Variance great readability 

---

### **How Do We Calculate Variance?**

The formula for variance is:
$$
\text{Var}(X) = E\left[(X - E[X])^2\right]
$$
This means we take the expected value of the squared deviations from the mean.

A simplified formula for discrete random variables:
$$
\text{Var}(X) = E[X^2] - (E[X])^2
$$

Where:
- $E[X^2]$: The expected value of $X^2$
- $(E[X])^2$: The square of the expected value of $X$

---

### **Example Using Your Data**

From the previous calculation, we know:
- $E[X] = 1.20$

| $X$ | $P(X)$ | $X^2$ | $X^2 \cdot P(X)$ |
|--------|------------|-----------|----------------------|
| 0      | 0.16       | 0         | 0                   |
| 1      | 0.48       | 1         | 0.48                |
| 2      | 0.36       | 4         | 1.44                |

$$
E[X^2] = \sum X^2 \cdot P(X) = 0 + 0.48 + 1.44 = 1.92
$$

Now, calculate variance:
$$
\text{Var}(X) = E[X^2] - (E[X])^2
$$
$$
\text{Var}(X) = 1.92 - (1.20)^2
$$
$$
\text{Var}(X) = 1.92 - 1.44 
= \boxed {0.48}
$$

---

### **What Does This Tell Us?**
The variance of 0.48 indicates that the outcomes of $X$ are not very widely spread around the mean (1.20). This compactness helps in understanding the reliability of the expected value and assessing variability.
