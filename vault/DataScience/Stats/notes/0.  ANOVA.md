
## 🧠 What is ANOVA?

**Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)** is a statistical method used to compare **means across multiple groups** to determine if observed differences are due to actual effects or random variation.

---

## 🎯 Why Use ANOVA?

- A **t-test** works for comparing two groups.
- But when comparing **three or more groups**, multiple t-tests increase the risk of **Type I errors**.
- ANOVA allows testing **all group means simultaneously**, controlling error rates.

---

## 🔬 Mathematical Foundation

ANOVA is based on **variance decomposition**:
- **Total variance** = variance **between groups** (treatment effect) + variance **within groups** (random error)
- It uses the **F-statistic** to assess whether the between-group variance is significantly greater than within-group variance.

> Assumes:
> - Data is **normally distributed**
> - **Equal variances** across groups

---

## 📐 One-Way ANOVA

- Tests one factor (independent variable) with multiple levels.
- Example: comparing plant growth using three fertilizers (A, B, C).

### Hypotheses:
- $H_0$: All group means are equal
- $H_1$: At least one group mean differs

### Application:
Use when testing **one factor** like:
- Teaching methods
- Drug dosages
- Machine settings

---

## 📐 Two-Way ANOVA

- Tests two factors **and their interaction**.
- Example: Fertilizer type (A, B) and watering schedule (daily, weekly)

### Tests Three Hypotheses:
1. Effect of Factor A (e.g., fertilizer)
2. Effect of Factor B (e.g., watering)
3. Interaction between A and B

### Application:
Use when two variables may **interact**, e.g.:
- Teaching method × class size
- Drug type × gender

---

## 🔁 Differences Between One-Way and Two-Way ANOVA

| Feature           | One-Way ANOVA               | Two-Way ANOVA                             |
|-------------------|------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| Factors           | 1                            | 2                                          |
| Interaction Test  | ❌ No                        | ✅ Yes                                     |
| Complexity        | Lower                        | Higher                                     |
| Example           | Diet type effect             | Diet × Exercise interaction effect         |

---

## 🧪 How ANOVA Works in Practice

1. Define hypotheses (equal means vs. differences).
2. Calculate **between-group** and **within-group** variance.
3. Compute **F-statistic**.
4. Compare with F-table critical value.
5. If significant → reject $H_0$, investigate further (e.g., post-hoc tests).

---

## 🧠 When to Use

- ✅ **One-Way ANOVA**: Comparing multiple levels of **a single factor**  
  → e.g., effects of 3 diets on weight loss

- ✅ **Two-Way ANOVA**: Studying **two factors** and their **interaction**  
  → e.g., diet + exercise on weight loss
