Kurtosis is a measure used in statistics to describe the peakedness  of a distribution
- **Low kurtosis** means the tails are thinner, with fewer extreme values (outliers). The data is more evenly spread out.
- **High kurtosis** means the tails are fatter, meaning there are more extreme values (outliers) in the data.
- Formula:
	- from [[Moment#^cde9b9|Moment]] 
	$$\gamma_2 = \frac{\mu_4}{\sigma^4} - 3$$
## Types of Kurtosis
![[Pasted image 20241006200635.png]]
1. **Mesokurtic (Kurtosis ≈ 0):** 
	 - This is the kurtosis of a normal distribution. 
	 - The tails of the distribution are similar to those of a normal distribution. 
	 - Example: Standard normal distribution (Gaussian distribution). 
1. **Leptokurtic (Kurtosis > 0)**:  
	-  Distributions with positive kurtosis have more pronounced peaks and fatter tails.
	-  There are more extreme outliers than in a normal distribution.
	-  Example: Data showing higher peak and thicker tails compared to a normal distribution. 
1. **Platykurtic (Kurtosis < 0)**:
	-  Distributions with negative kurtosis have flatter peaks and thinner tails. 
	-  Fewer extreme outliers than in a normal distribution.
	-  example: Uniform distribution, where data is spread more evenly
