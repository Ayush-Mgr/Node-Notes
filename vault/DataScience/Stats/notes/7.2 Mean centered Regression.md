- It is the estimation or the prediction of the average value of one variable $y$ dependent on another  $x$ 
- estimation is done by using a equation on the basis of [[2.Bivariate data]]  This equation is called _regression equation_,  And it's geometrical representation is called _regression curve_.

## linear regression
- In linear regression, the relationship of variables is assumed to be linear straight line
- Estimate of  $y$  is obtained from the following equation
 
$$\hat{y}-\bar{y} = b_{yx}(x-\bar{x})$$
where:
* $\bar{y}$: mean of the dependent variable $y$

* $\bar{x}$: mean of the independent variable $x$

* $b_{yx}$: regression coefficient (same as the slope $b_1$)

* $\hat{y}$: predicted value from regression

## linear regression equation 

$$\hat{y}-\bar{y} = b_{yx}(x-\bar{x})$$ ^96d7ab
### The **regression coefficient** $b_{yx}$

^eb0056

- The **regression coefficient of  $y$  on  $x$  $b_{yx}$ measures the *average change* in  $y$  for a *unit change in *$x$* . 
	- It is calculated as:
		$$b_{yx} = \frac{Cov(x, y)}{Var(x)} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^n (x_i - \bar{x})(y_i - \bar{y})}{\sum_{i=1}^n (x_i - \bar{x})^2}$$
	- it has units of *units of  $y$  divided by units of  $x$ *. It is important to note that $b_{yx}$ is dependent on the scales of  $x$  and  $y$  .
## proporties 
1. There are two line regression equations:
	 1. Regression. equation of $y$ on $x$ .
		$$y=\bar{y}=b_{yx}(x-\bar{x})$$
	 2. Regression equation of $x$ on $y$ .
		 $$x=\bar{x}=b_{xy}(y-\bar{y})$$
	 note :$b_{yx}$ & $b_{xy}$is different in both equation
	1.$$b_{yx} = \frac{Cov(x, y)}{Var(x)}=r\frac{ \sigma_y }{ \sigma_x }$$
	2.$$b_{xy} = \frac{Cov(x, y)}{Var(y)}=r\frac{ \sigma_x }{ \sigma_y }$$
2. The product of the two regression coefficients is  equal to the square of [[6. Correlation coefficient (r)|correlation coefficient]]
	-$$b_{yx}.b_{xy}=r^2$$
3. $r,b_{yx}$ & $b_{xy}= 0$ if $r=0$ 
4.  if line intersects at $(\bar{x},\bar{y})$, then the slopes of regression line of $x$ on $y$ and the regression line of $y$ on $x$ respectively, $1/b_{xy}$ and $b_{yx}$
5.  the angle between the two regression lines depend on the coefficient of correlation($r$) when$r=0$the two lines are perpendicular to each other when$r=+1$ or $r=-1$ they collide
6.   the two regression equations are usually different , 
	-  but when $r=+-1$ exactly, they become identical , inversely 
	- when$r = 0$, the regression equation reduce to recreation $x=\bar{x}$and$y=\bar{y}$,  neither $x$ nor $y$ can be estimated from linear regression equation since they don't have any coefficient of correlation

### example
### Solving for Regression of X on Y

Given data:
-  $\Sigma X = 24$ 
-  $\Sigma Y = 44$ 
-  $\Sigma XY = 306$ 
-  $\Sigma X^2 = 164$ 
-  $\Sigma Y^2 = 574$ 
-  $n = 4$ 

#### Step 1: Calculate the Means  $\bar{X}$  and  $\bar{Y}$ 
The means are given by:
$$ \bar{X} = \frac{\Sigma X}{n} = \frac{24}{4} = 6 $$
$$ \bar{Y} = \frac{\Sigma Y}{n} = \frac{44}{4} = 11 $$

#### Step 2: Calculate the Regression Coefficient  $b_{xy}$ 
The regression coefficient  $b_{xy}$  is given by:
$$
b_{xy} = \frac{\Sigma XY - n \cdot \bar{X} \cdot \bar{Y}}{\Sigma Y^2 - n \cdot \bar{Y}^2}
$$
Substitute the given values:
$$
b_{xy} = \frac{306 - 4 \cdot 6 \cdot 11}{574 - 4 \cdot 11^2}
$$
Simplify:
$$ 306 - 4 \cdot 6 \cdot 11 = 306 - 264 = 42 $$
$$ 574 - 4 \cdot 11^2 = 574 - 484 = 90 $$
$$
b_{xy} = \frac{42}{90} = 0.4667
$$

#### Step 3: Regression Equation of X on Y
The regression equation is:
$$
x - \bar{X} = b_{xy}(y - \bar{Y})
$$
Substitute  $\bar{X} = 6$ ,  $\bar{Y} = 11$ , and  $b_{xy} = 0.4667$ :
$$
x - 6 = 0.4667(y - 11)
$$
Simplify:
$$
x = 6 + 0.4667(y - 11)
$$
$$
x = 6 + 0.4667y - 5.1337
$$
$$
x = 0.4667y + 0.8663
$$

#### Step 4: Estimate   $x$   When  $y = 6$ 
Substitute  $y = 6$  into the regression equation:
$$
x = 0.4667 \cdot 6 + 0.8663
$$
$$ x = 2.8 + 0.8663 = 3.6663 $$

### Final Answer:
The estimated value of   $x$   when  $y = 6$  is:
$$
\boxed{3.67}
$$
