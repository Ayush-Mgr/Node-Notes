
# The Viterbi Algorithm: A Comprehensive Guide

_Based on the explanation by [ritvikmath](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IqXdjdOgXPM&t=29s)_

## 1. Introduction and Context

The Viterbi algorithm is a dynamic programming algorithm used to find the most likely sequence of hidden states (the "Viterbi path") that results in a sequence of observed events.

In the context of **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**, it is the standard method for **Part of Speech (POS) Tagging**using Hidden Markov Models (HMM).

### The Goal

We want to assign the correct Part of Speech (Noun, Verb, Determiner, etc.) to every word in the sentence:

> **"the fans watch the race"**

While humans can intuitively tell that "watch" is a verb here, a computer sees ambiguity. "Watch" can be a noun (a wristwatch) or a verb (to look). "Fans" can be a noun (supporters) or a verb (to blow air).

## 2. The Problem Setup

To make the example tractable, the video simplifies the universe of possibilities:

1. **The Sentence:** $W = [\text{"the", "fans", "watch", "the", "race"}]$
    
2. **Possible Tags (Hidden States):**
    
    - **Determiner (D):** Fixed for the word "the".
        
    - **Noun (N) or Verb (V):** Options for "fans", "watch", "race".
        
3. **Search Space:**
    
    - Since "fans", "watch", and "race" each have 2 options, there are $2 \times 2 \times 2 = 8$ possible combinations of tags.
        
    - **Brute Force approach:** Calculate probabilities for all 8 paths and pick the highest. This is $O(P^L)$(exponential time), where $P$ is the number of tags and $L$ is sentence length. This becomes impossible for long sentences.
        

### The Hidden Markov Model (HMM) Components

The HMM relies on two probability matrices derived from training data:

1. **Transition Probabilities (**$A$**):** The probability of moving from one POS tag to another (e.g., $P(\text{Noun} | \text{Determiner})$).
    
    - _Rule:_ Rows must sum to 1.
        
2. **Emission Probabilities (**$B$**):** The probability of a specific word being generated by a POS tag (e.g., $P(\text{"fans"} | \text{Noun})$).
    
    - _Note:_ Rows do not sum to 1 in this simplified example because we are only looking at a subset of English words.

The Viterbi algorithm avoids brute force by building a "trellis" and pruning low-probability paths early. We calculate the probability of being in a specific state at a specific step, keeping only the best path to get there.

### Step 1: Start $\to$ Word 1 ("the")

- **Observation:** "the"
    
- **Tag:** Determiner (D)
    
- **Calculation:**
    
    $$P(\text{Start} \to D) \times P(\text{"the"} | D)$$$$0.8 \times 0.2 = \mathbf{0.16}$$
- **State:** We are at "the"/Det with score **0.16**.
    

### Step 2: Word 2 ("fans")

"Fans" can be a Noun or a Verb. We calculate probabilities for both branches extending from Step 1.

- **Option A: "fans" is a Noun**
    
    - Previous Score ($0.16$) $\times$ Transition ($D \to N$) $\times$ Emission ($N \to \text{"fans"}$)
        
    - $0.16 \times 0.9 \times 0.1 = \mathbf{0.0144}$
        
- **Option B: "fans" is a Verb**
    
    - Previous Score ($0.16$) $\times$ Transition ($D \to V$) $\times$ Emission ($V \to \text{"fans"}$)
        
    - $0.16 \times 0.1 \times 0.2 = \mathbf{0.0032}$
        

**Status:** We keep **BOTH** values. We cannot discard the lower value ($0.0032$) yet because the optimal path might genuinely go through the verb "fans" depending on what happens next.

### Step 3: Word 3 ("watch") - The "Busiest Junction"

This is the core logic of Viterbi. "Watch" can be a Noun or Verb. We must determine the best way to reach "watch" as a Noun, and the best way to reach "watch" as a Verb.

#### Scenario 3.1: Reaching "watch" as a NOUN

There are two ways to arrive at "watch" (Noun):

1. **From "fans" (Noun):** * Score: $0.0144 \times P(N \to N) \times P(N \to \text{"watch"})$
    
    - $0.0144 \times 0.5 \times 0.3 = \mathbf{0.00216}$
        
2. **From "fans" (Verb):**
    
    - Score: $0.0032 \times P(V \to N) \times P(N \to \text{"watch"})$
        
    - $0.0032 \times 0.5 \times 0.3 = \mathbf{0.00048}$
        

**The Viterbi Logic (Pruning):** We have two paths converging on the exact same node ("watch"/Noun). Since both paths end at the same place, the future possibilities are identical for both. Therefore, we only need to remember the path that got us here with the _highest_ probability.

- **Compare:** $0.00216$ vs $0.00048$
    
- **Action:** Keep $0.00216$. **Discard** the path coming from "fans" (Verb). We effectively delete the memory of the weaker path.
    

#### Scenario 3.2: Reaching "watch" as a VERB

We repeat the logic for the Verb state.

1. **From "fans" (Noun):** Score $\approx 0.00108$
    
2. **From "fans" (Verb):** Score $= 0$ (Transition prob was 0)
    

- **Action:** Keep the path from "fans" (Noun).
    

### Step 4: Word 4 ("the")

We propagate forward from our winning survivors at Step 3.

- Transition from "watch" (Noun) to "the" (Det) is 0.
    
- Transition from "watch" (Verb) to "the" (Det) is viable.
    
- We calculate the score and prune any zero-probability paths.
    

### Step 5: Word 5 ("race") & Backtracking

Finally, we calculate scores for "race" being a Noun or a Verb.

- **Result:** The probability for "race" being a Noun is significantly higher ($9.72 \times 10^{-6}$) than it being a Verb ($3.24 \times 10^{-6}$).
    
- **Backtracking:**
    
    1. Best End State: **Noun** ("race")
        
    2. Where did we come from to get this best score? **Determiner** ("the")
        
    3. Where did we come from to get there? **Verb** ("watch")
        
    4. Where did we come from to get there? **Noun** ("fans")
        
    5. Start: **Determiner** ("the")
        

**Final Tag Sequence:** Determiner $\to$ Noun $\to$ Verb $\to$ Determiner $\to$ Noun. _(Matches human intuition: The fans [people] watch [action] the race [event])._

## 4. Computational Complexity: Why Viterbi Wins

The video emphasizes the "Big O" notation to prove why Viterbi is superior to Brute Force.

### Variables

- $L$**:** Length of the sentence (sequence length).
    
- $P$**:** Number of Part of Speech tags (hidden states).
    

### Brute Force Analysis

To check every single path, we branch $P$ times at every word.

- **Complexity:** $O(P^L)$
    
- **Growth:** Exponential. If $P=40$ (standard English tagset) and $L=20$, this is astronomically impossible.
    

### Viterbi Algorithm Analysis

At each step (word) in the sequence, we look at each of the $P$ current nodes. For each node, we look back at the $P$nodes from the previous step to find the best incoming connection.

- Operations per step: $P \times P = P^2$
    
- Total steps: $L$
    
- **Complexity:** $O(L \times P^2)$
    
- **Growth:** Linear with respect to sentence length. This is highly efficient.
    

### The "Aha!" Moment

Why is it faster? In brute force, we keep every failing path alive until the very end. In Viterbi, **we discard suboptimal paths at every single step.** If a path is losing at step 3, and it merges with a winning path, we know it can never "catch up" later, so we delete it immediately to save memory and computation.
