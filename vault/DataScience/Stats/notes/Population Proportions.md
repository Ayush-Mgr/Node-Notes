### ✅ **Population Proportions – Simple Definition:**

A **population proportion** is the **percentage (or fraction)** of individuals in a population that have a certain characteristic.

---

### 🔍 **Formula:**

$$P=\frac{\text{Number of individuals with the characteristic}}{\text{Total population size}}$$

---

### 📊 **Example:**

Suppose in a city of 1,000 people:

- 400 people like coffee
    

Then the **population proportion** P of coffee lovers is:

$P=\frac{400}{1000}=0.4$ or $40\%$

---

### 🧠 **Why It Matters:**

Population proportions are used when you want to:

- **Estimate** how many people have a trait (e.g., support a politician)
    
- **Compare** two groups (e.g., coffee lovers in City A vs. City B)
    
- Conduct **proportion hypothesis testing** (like the Z-test for proportions)
