##  **Bivariate Data**: 

**Bivariate data** is a type of data that involves two variables eg. , $x$ and $y$ , where $x$ could be weight and $y$ could be hight  . These variables are often paired together, meaning that for each value of one variable, there's a corresponding value for the other variable , for  $x_1$ there is $y_1$  $(x_1 , y_1)....(x_n , y_n)$.

**Key Characteristics:**

- **Two Variables:** The core element is the presence of two distinct variables.
- **Paired Observations:** Each data point consists of a pair of values, one for each variable.
- **Relationships:** The primary focus of bivariate analysis is to investigate the relationship between these two variables.


## **Bivariate Frequency Distribution Table**

^230282

A bivariate frequency distribution table, also known as a two-way frequency table or contingency table, is a statistical tool used to display the 
frequency of joint occurrences of two variables. It's particularly useful for analyzing categorical data.

**Structure:**

- **Rows:** Represent the categories or levels of one variable.
- **Columns:** Represent the categories or levels of the other variable.
- **Cells:** Each cell contains the frequency of the combination of the corresponding row and column categories.

### MARGINAL DISTRIBUTION 

^98ac33

- it is the total of frequency distribution of  $x$ variable or $y$ variable only 
	- and the  $\sum{f}$ of  x or y is called marginal distribution 

| Height Class (cm) $Y$ | frequency      |
| --------------------- | -------------- |
| 140-150               | 25             |
| 150-160               | 52             |
| 160-170               | 56             |
| 170-180               | 42             |
| Total                 | $\sum{f}=$ 175 |
$\sum{f}=$ 175 is the marginal distribution  of $Y$'s data

### CONDITIONAL DISTRIBUTION
- it is the frequency distribution of  $x$ variable or $y$ variable on certain condition  
- eg.(if x= hight and y = weight , if we want frequency distribution of x  on the basis of  weight's frequency  lets say 50 - 60 kgs    )
- $\sum{f}=$ CONDITIONAL DISTRIBUTION
