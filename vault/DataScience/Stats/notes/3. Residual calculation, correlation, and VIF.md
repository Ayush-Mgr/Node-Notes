## 3. Residual Calculation

Predicted values using the regression equation:

$$
\hat{Y} = 3 + 2X_1 + 0X_2
$$

For each data point:

- $\hat{Y}_1 = 3 + 2(1) + 0(2) = 5$
- $\hat{Y}_2 = 3 + 2(2) + 0(3) = 7$
- $\hat{Y}_3 = 3 + 2(3) + 0(4) = 9$

## 4. Calculation of $R^2$

^268c6a

$$
R^2 = 1 - \frac{SSE}{SST} = 1 - \frac{0}{20} = 1
$$

- Sum of Squared Total (SST):

$$SST = (y_i-\bar{y})$$


- Mean of $Y$  $(\bar{Y})$:

$$
\bar{Y} = \frac{5 + 7 + 9 + 11}{4} = 8
$$

- Total Sum of Squares (SST):

$$
SST = (5 - 8)^2 + (7 - 8)^2 + (9 - 8)^2 + (11 - 8)^2 = 9 + 1 + 1 + 9 = 20
$$

- Residual Sum of Squares (SSE) :

$$
e = Y - \hat{Y} =
\begin{bmatrix}
5 - 5 \\
7 - 7 \\
9 - 9 \\
11 - 11 \\
\end{bmatrix} =
\begin{bmatrix}
0 \\
0 \\
0 \\
0 \\
\end{bmatrix}
$$

$$SSE = (0^2+0^2+0^2+0^2)=0$$


**Correlation $(R^2)$** :

$$
R^2 = 1 - \frac{SSE}{SST} = 1 - \frac{0}{20} = 1
$$

**Interpretation**: $R^2 = 1$, indicating a perfect model fit.

## 5. Variance Inflation Factor (VIF)

### VIF Formula:

$$
VIF(X_j) = \frac{1}{1 - R_j^2}
$$

### VIF for $X_1$

Regress $X_1$ on $X_2$:

Given:

$$
X_1 = \begin{bmatrix}
1 \\
2 \\
3 \\
4 \\
\end{bmatrix}, \quad
X_2 = \begin{bmatrix}
2 \\
3 \\
4 \\
6 \\
\end{bmatrix}
$$

Linear regression result: $X_1 = 1 + X_2$

So, $R^2 = 1$:

$$
VIF(X_1) = \frac{1}{1 - 1} = \infty
$$

### VIF for $X_2$

Regress $X_2$ on $X_1$:

Linear regression result: $X_2 = 1 + X_1$

So, $R^2 = 1$:

$$
VIF(X_2) = \frac{1}{1 - 1} = \infty
$$

#### **Step 1: Regression of $X_1$ on $X_2$**

Assume the linear model:

$$
X_1 = \alpha_0 + \alpha_1 X_2 + \epsilon
$$

To find $R^2$, we compute:

* $\bar{X}_1 = \frac{1+2+3+4}{4} = 2.5$
* $\bar{X}_2 = \frac{2+2+5+7}{4} = 4$

Now compute:

* Covariance:

$$
\text{Cov}(X_1, X_2) = \frac{1}{n} \sum (X_{1i} - \bar{X}_1)(X_{2i} - \bar{X}_2)$$
$$= \frac{1}{4} [(1-2.5)(2-4) + (2-2.5)(2-4) + (3-2.5)(5-4) + (4-2.5)(7-4)]
$$

$$
= \frac{1}{4} [(-1.5)(-2) + (-0.5)(-2) + (0.5)(1) + (1.5)(3)] \\
= \frac{1}{4} [3 + 1 + 0.5 + 4.5] = \frac{9}{4} = 2.25
$$

* Variance of $X_2$:

$$
\text{Var}(X_2) = \frac{1}{4}[(2-4)^2 + (2-4)^2 + (5-4)^2 + (7-4)^2] \\
= \frac{1}{4}[4 + 4 + 1 + 9] = \frac{18}{4} = 4.5
$$

Now slope $\alpha_1$:

$$
\alpha_1 = \frac{\text{Cov}(X_1, X_2)}{\text{Var}(X_2)} = \frac{2.25}{4.5} = 0.5
$$

Intercept:

$$
\alpha_0 = \bar{X}_1 - \alpha_1 \bar{X}_2 = 2.5 - 0.5(4) = 0.5
$$

So regression equation:

$$
\hat{X}_1 = 0.5 + 0.5 X_2
$$

---

#### **Step 2: Compute $R^2$**

$$
R^2 = 1 - \frac{\text{SSR}}{\text{SST}}
$$

* $\text{SST} = \sum (X_{1i} - \bar{X}_1)^2 = (-1.5)^2 + (-0.5)^2 + (0.5)^2 + (1.5)^2 \\= 2.25 + 0.25 + 0.25 + 2.25 = 5$

* Predicted $\hat{X}_1 = [1.5, 1.5, 3.0, 4.0]$

* Residuals = Actual - Predicted:

  * $1 - 1.5 = -0.5$
  * $2 - 1.5 = 0.5$
  * $3 - 3.0 = 0$
  * $4 - 4.0 = 0$

* $\text{SSR} = \sum (\text{residual})^2 = (-0.5)^2 + 0.5^2 + 0 + 0 = 0.25 + 0.25 = 0.5$

$$
R^2 = 1 - \frac{0.5}{5} = 0.9
$$

---

#### **Step 3: Compute VIF**

$$
VIF(X_1) = \frac{1}{1 - R^2} = \frac{1}{1 - 0.9} = \frac{1}{0.1} = 10
$$

$$
VIF(X_1) = 10

| **VIF Value**    | **Interpretation**                                                                        |
| ---------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **1**            | No multicollinearity. Independent variable is not correlated with other predictors.       |
| **1 ã 5**        | Moderate correlation. Usually acceptable.                                                 |
| **5 ã 10**       | Potentially problematic. Investigate further.                                             |
