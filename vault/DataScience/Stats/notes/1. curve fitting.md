- When two sets of observation are present, we could often find relationship between them . For example, height and weight of a person has independent relation, whereas yield of crop depends on the amount of rainfall so it has dependable relation . We could describe this with mathematical equation which gives a certain geometrical curve, and the process of finding search curve is called curve fitting
- some common types of equation to find curves are

| **Curve Name**                        | **Equation**                                            |
| ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Straight Line                         | $( y = mx + c )$                                        |
| Parabola                              | $( y = ax^2 + bx + c )$                                 |
| Cubic Curve                           | $( y = ax^3 + bx^2 + cx + d )$                          |
| Polynomial of the $( n^{th} )$ Degree | $( y = a_nx^n + a_{n-1}x^{n-1} + \ldots + a_1x + a_0 )$ |
| Exponential Curve                     | $( y = ae^{bx} )$                                       |
| Clove Modified Exponential Curve      | $( y = ae^{bx} + ce^{dx} )$                             |
| Logistic Curve                        | $( y = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(x-x_0)}} )$                   |
| Gompertz Curve                        | $( y = ae^{-be^{-cx}} )$                                |
| Geometric Curve                       | $( y = ar^x )$                                          |
- here variable $x$ is often called independent variable 
- variable $y$ is the dependent variable
- and every other variables, $a$ $b$ $c$ $d...p$ are constants
