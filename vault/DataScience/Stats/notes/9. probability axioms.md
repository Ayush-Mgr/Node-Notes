
The **[[axioms]] of probability** are a set of fundamental principles introduced by **Andrey Kolmogorov** to define a rigorous mathematical framework for probability theory. These axioms form the basis of modern probability theory and are as follows:

---

### **1. Non-Negativity Axiom**
For any event $A$ in the sample space $S$, the probability of $A$, denoted as $P(A)$, is always non-negative:
$$
P(A) \geq 0
$$
This means probabilities cannot be negative.

---

### **2. Normalization Axiom**
The probability of the entire sample space $S$ is equal to 1:
$$
P(S) = 1
$$
This ensures that the probability of all possible outcomes adds up to 1.

---

### **3. Additivity Axiom (Disjoint Events)**
If $A$ and $B$ are mutually exclusive (disjoint) events, meaning $A \cap B = \emptyset$, then the probability of $A \cup B$ is the sum of the probabilities of $A$ and $B$:
$$
P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B)
$$
This extends to any finite or countable collection of mutually exclusive events.
