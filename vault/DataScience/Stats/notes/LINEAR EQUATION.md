### **Understanding a Linear Equation in $n$ Variables**

A **linear equation** in $n$ variables $x_1, x_2, x_3, ..., x_n$ has the general form: (every linear equations have this form ) 
- linear equations together forms the [[system of linear equations]]

$$
a_1 x_1 + a_2 x_2 + a_3 x_3 + \dots + a_n x_n = b
$$

where:
- $a_1, a_2, ..., a_n$ (real numbers) are the **[[coefficients]]** of $x_1....x_n$ ,
- $b$ is the **constant term** (also a real number),
- $x_1, x_2, ..., x_n$ are the **variables**. _(unknowns we need to solve for)_

### **Key Terms Explained**
1. **Leading Coefficient ($a_1$)**:  
   - This is the **first nonzero coefficient** in the equation when written in standard form.
   - It represents the weight or importance of the first variable in the entire equation.
   - Example: In $3x_1 + 5x_2 - 2x_3 = 7$, the leading coefficient is **3**.

1. **Leading Variable ($x_1$)**:  
   - This is the **variable associated with the leading coefficient**.
   - It is usually the first variable appearing in the equation when written in standard form.
   - Example: In $3x_1 + 5x_2 - 2x_3 = 7$, the leading variable is **$x_1$**.

### **Example 1: A Linear Equation with 3 Variables**
$$
4x_1 - 2x_2 + 7x_3 = 10
$$
- **Leading coefficient** = $4$ (since it is the first nonzero coefficient).
- **Leading variable** = $x_1$.
--- 
### **Example 2: A Linear Equation with 4 Variables**
$$
0x_1 + 5x_2 - 3x_3 + 2x_4 = 8
$$
- Here, $x_1$ is missing (coefficient is 0).
- **Leading coefficient** = $5$ (first nonzero coefficient).
- **Leading variable** = $x_2$ (since $x_1$ is absent).


### **Conclusion**
- A **linear equation** is simply an equation where the variables appear only to the _**first power**_ (no exponents, square roots, etc.).
- The **leading coefficient** and **leading variable** help identify the most significant term in an equation.
- These concepts are important when solving systems of equations using **Gaussian elimination**, where equations are systematically rearranged.
