- When $\hat{y}$ is the estimated value of $y$ from the [[7.2 Mean centered Regression#^96d7ab|regression equation]] then it can be shown$$\sum({y_i-\bar{y})^2=\sum(y_i-\hat{y_i})+\sum(\hat{y_i}-\bar{y_i})^2}$$
	- $\sum ( y_i-\bar{y}) ^2$  is _Total variation_ (also called **Total Sum of Squares, SST**).
	- $\sum(y_i-\hat{y_i})^2$ is [[4.Method of Least Square|SSE]] or _unexplained variation_
	- $\sum(\hat{y_i}-\bar{y_i})^2$ is Variation due to regression or _Explained  variation_ , or **Sum of Squares for Regression, SSR**.
-  new interpretation of the square of [[6. Correlation coefficient (r)|correlation coefficient]]
	- $$r^2 = \frac{\text{Explained Variation}}{\text{Total Variation}}$$
	- proportion of total variation explained by regression $=r^2$
	- **INRERPRETATION**
		- $r^2 = +1$ , result of  regression perfectly aligns with the given data , the independent variable perfectly explains the dependable data 
		- $r^2 =  0$ , result of  regression dose not  aligns with the given data , no relation between data
