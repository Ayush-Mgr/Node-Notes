## EXPLANATION ALGo


*"Let's say, here we want to reach from the **green point** to **red**, using **A-star algorithm**."*  

The **A-star algorithm** uses this formula: **F equals to H(n) plus G(n)**.  

*"Now, what does that mean?"*  

Here, **G(n)** is the cost of moving from one block to another. *In our condition, this is a constant!* *No matter where you go—* **up, down, left, and  right**—the cost is **always one**.  

And **H(n)** is the **heuristic value**, which every node has. *It tells us how far the node is from the end!* It is found using this *fancy formula* called **Manhattan distance**—which basically calculates the **distance between our current node and the end node**.  

*"Now, combining this whole soup together..."* *gives us the* **F value**.  

*"Alright, let's get started!"*  

Now, suppose we start from the **green point**.  
We calculate the **F value** by summing:  
- The cost of moving (**G**), which is **none** at the start.  
- The heuristic value (**H**), which is the **distance** to the goal.

next

*"Let's say we move up..."*  
- We get its **G value**—since it’s a **constant**, it’s just **1**.  
- We calculate its **heuristic value** as well.  
- Then, we **add them together** to get its **F value**.  

And we repeat this *again and again*—checking every direction: **down, left, and right**.  
*"Then comes the key step!"* We choose the **lowest F value** and move **towards it**.  

repeat it again , get its neighbors and their f value and choose the lowest one and move towards it   

And we keep **repeating this process**... *until finally...*  

*"We reach the END—"* **the red point!** 🎯
