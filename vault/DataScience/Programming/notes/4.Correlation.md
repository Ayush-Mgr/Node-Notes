- When data have different units, *relationship* between them or strength of association between this variables, it is called correlation
- if the variation of one variable depends on the other, it is called  related *correlated*
	- if $y$ increase as$X$ increase, it is called _positively correlated_ if
	- $y$ decrease as the $X$ increase it is called _negatively correlated_, 
	- and if $y$ remain constant, no matter how $X$ increase or decrease. It is called _uncorrelated_
- if the amount of change in one variable $x$ is a constant ratio to the amount of change in another variable $y$, then the correlation is said to be _linear correlation_ or _simple correlation_ [[3. scatter diagram#^4ec4b5|Example on plot]].
	- it is measured by correlation coefficient
