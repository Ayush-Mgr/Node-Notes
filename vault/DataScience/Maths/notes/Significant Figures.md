- They **show the precision** of measurements.
- Used in **scientific calculations** to avoid giving misleading accuracy.
--- 
### **Rules for Counting Significant Figures**

1. **Nonzero digits are always significant.**
    
    - **Example:** 12341234 has **4 significant figures**.
2. **Zeros between nonzero digits are significant.**
    
    - **Example:** 10031003 has **4 significant figures**.
3. **Leading zeros (zeros before nonzero digits) are NOT significant.**
    
    - **Example:** 0.00450.0045 has **2 significant figures** (4 and 5).
4. **Trailing zeros (zeros at the end) are significant IF there is a decimal point.**
    
    - **Example:**
        - 25002500 → **2 significant figures** (uncertain precision).
        - 2500.02500.0 → **5 significant figures** (precise measurement).
        - 2.5002.500 → **4 significant figures** (because of the decimal).

---

### **Rounding to Significant Figures**

- If rounding to **3 significant figures**,
    - 3.141593.14159 → 3.143.14
    - 0.0045670.004567 → 0.004570.00457
