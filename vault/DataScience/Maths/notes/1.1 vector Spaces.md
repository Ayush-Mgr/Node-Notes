## in plain words
A vector space  just a [[2. Groups|Groups]] with additional operation which allow us to multiply the vectors with Scaler and follows additional properties of Abelian group under addition. This means:
	that vector addition must be commutative (x + y = y + x) 
	associative ((x + y) + z = x + (y + z)).

---
A **vector space** is a **set of objects**, where each object can be a **real number, polynomial, matrix, function**, or anything else that satisfies specific properties.  

## Properties of a Vector Space  

1. **Closure under addition**  
   - The sum of two objects in the set must also be within the set.  

2. **Closure under scalar multiplication**  
   - Multiplying an object by any scalar (real number) must result in an object that is still within the set.  

## Examples  

### **Example 1: Real Numbers**  

1. Addition of real numbers:  
$$
1 + 1 = 2
$$  
Since $2$ is also a real number, the set of real numbers is closed under addition.  

2. Scalar multiplication of a real number:  
$$
3 \times 2 = 6
$$  
Since $6$ is still a real number, the set of real numbers is closed under scalar multiplication.  

### **Example 2: Polynomials**  

1. Addition of two polynomials:  

If we add $Q(x) = x + 1$ to $P(x) = x^2 + 2x + 3$:  
$$
(x^2 + 2x + 3) + (x + 1) = x^2 + 3x + 4
$$  
Since the result is still a polynomial, the set of polynomials is closed under addition.  

2. Scalar multiplication of a polynomial:  

Multiplying $P(x) = x^2 + 2x + 3$ by a scalar $k = 2$:  
$$
2 \times (x^2 + 2x + 3) = 2x^2 + 4x + 6
$$  
Since the result is still a polynomial, the set of polynomials is closed under scalar multiplication.  

---

A vector space is a **mathematical structure** where elements follow the rules of **addition and scalar multiplication**, ensuring that the set remains closed under these operations.
