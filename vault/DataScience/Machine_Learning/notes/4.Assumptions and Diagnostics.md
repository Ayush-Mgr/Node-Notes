Multiple regression models rely on several key assumptions to ensure valid, interpretable, and reliable results. Among the most important diagnostics are:

---

### 1. 🧩 **Collinearity (Multicollinearity)**

#### 📌 What is it?

- When **two or more independent variables are highly correlated** with each other.
    
- Makes it hard to determine the **individual effect** of each variable on the dependent variable.
#### Example

such as the US dollars and Indian rupees, they have constant value if one increase and another one also increase in a constant weight, which means they are very high correlation. It is better to drop one currency

#### 🎯 Why is it a problem?

- Inflates **standard errors** of the coefficients.
    
- Makes **coefficient estimates unstable** and unreliable.
    
- Can result in **wrong signs or magnitudes** for variables.
	
-  When features are highly correlated, the model can’t tell which one is really responsible for changes in the dependent variable.
    

#### 🔍 How to detect it?

- **Variance Inflation Factor (VIF)**:
    
    - VIF > 5 or 10 indicates problematic multicollinearity.
        
- **Correlation matrix** of features.
    

#### 🛠 How to fix it?

- Remove or combine highly correlated variables.
    
- Use dimensionality reduction (e.g., PCA).
    
- Try regularization (e.g., **Ridge regression**).
    

---

### 2. 📈 **Heteroscedasticity**

#### 📌 What is it?

- The **variance of residuals is not constant** across all levels of the independent variables, or the residuals did not follow a trend upward trend or downward.
	where 
	- Residuals = $y− \hat{y}$​ (actual minus predicted)
- Violates the assumption of **homoscedasticity** (equal variance of errors).

or 

- **Heteroscedasticity means the errors (residuals) have unequal spread** across different values of your predictors or predicted values.
    
- It **often happens when the model isn’t capturing the data perfectly**, but it’s not _only_ about poor overall fit.
    
- Even a model with a decent correlation can have heteroscedasticity if the error variance changes systematically — for example, errors might get bigger as predicted values increase.
    

#### 🎯 Why is it a problem?

- Leads to **inefficient estimates**.
    
- Standard errors are biased → affects **hypothesis testing and confidence intervals**.
    

#### 🔍 How to detect it?

- **Plot residuals vs. predicted values**: look for a funnel shape or pattern.
    
- **Breusch-Pagan test** or **White test**.
    

#### 🛠 How to fix it?

- Transform the dependent variable (e.g., log(Y)).
    
- Use **robust standard errors**.
    
- Use **weighted least squares**.
    

---

### 3. 🔁 **Autocorrelation**

#### 📌 What is it?

- When residuals are **correlated with each other** — mostly a problem in **time-series data** or A single feature is **correlated with itself over time**
    
- Violates the assumption of **independent errors**.

#### EXAMPLE
Market correction,
(_it is bad because it is sudden and does not follow the fact, and the model follows the facts and model tries to predict trend, whereas the market has gone down due to correction_)

**Fluctuations (ups and downs) cause errors the model can’t handle**:

- Let’s say mood is trending up overall, but **it dips on some days**.
    
- Linear regression draws a **straight line** through the trend, but those dips cause the prediction to be **wrong on those days**.
    
- The model doesn’t understand **"momentum" or "seasonal swing"** — it just sees the average trend.

#### 🎯 Why is it a problem?

- Causes **biased standard errors**, leading to incorrect inferences.
    
- Reduces the predictive power of the model.
    

#### 🔍 How to detect it?

- **Durbin-Watson test** (value ≈ 2 is ideal; <1.5 or >2.5 indicates autocorrelation).
    
- Residual plots showing clear patterns over time.
    

#### 🛠 How to fix it?

- Add **lagged variables** (e.g., Y(t−1)) to the model.
    
- Use **time-series models** (e.g., ARIMA).
    
- Try **Generalized Least Squares (GLS)** instead of OLS.
    

---

## 🧾 Summary Table

| Assumption             | Problem Caused                     | Detection Method                 | Remedy                             |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| **Collinearity**       | Unstable coefficients, high SE     | VIF, correlation matrix          | Remove or combine variables, Ridge |
| **Heteroscedasticity** | Biased SE, unreliable inference    | Residual plots, BP/White tests   | Log transform, robust SE, WLS      |
| **Autocorrelation**    | Biased SE, invalid test statistics | Durbin-Watson, residual patterns | Add lags, use ARIMA or GLS         |
