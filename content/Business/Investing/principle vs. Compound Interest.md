### **Principal vs. Compound Interest**  

1. **Principal Amount**  
   - The original sum of money borrowed or invested.  
   - It remains the base amount on which interest is calculated.  
   - Example: If you take a loan of **$100**, the principal is **$100**.  

2. **Compound Interest**  
   - Interest is calculated on both the **principal** and the **previously accrued interest**.  
   - It grows over time, making the debt or investment increase exponentially.  
   - Example: If you have **$100** at **10% annual interest**, after:  
     - **Year 1:** $100 × 1.1 = **$110**  
     - **Year 2:** $110 × 1.1 = **$121**  

🔹 **Key Difference:** Principal remains constant unless repaid, while compound interest keeps increasing as interest is added to the total amount.
