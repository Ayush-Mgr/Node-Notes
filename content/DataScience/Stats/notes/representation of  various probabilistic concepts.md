

- **$P(A)$ :** denotes probability of occurrence of event A ^6e8f69
- $P(\bar{A})$ : denotes the non-occurrence of event A or event complementary to A or  opposite to A ^0551df
- $P(A+B)$ or $P(A\cup B)$ : the notes probability of _occurrence of at least one_ of these event A or B, A or B [[2.Terminologies#^99598a|mutually exclusive]] event. ^961955
- $P(AB)$ or$P(A \cap B)$: probability of  occurring both event A and B _at same time_ or also called compound event / compound probability
- $P(A/B)$: it is called [[5.theorem of probability#^f4cc59|conditional probability]]. It shows probability of occurrence of A assuming _B has actually occurred._
