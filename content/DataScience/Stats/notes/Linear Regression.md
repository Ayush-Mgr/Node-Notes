## Fitting a Line to Data(*least squared*)
manual method 
	**step**:
	1. draw linear lines at all possible different angles 
	2. find the residuals of linear lines at a specific angle
	3. square and sum each and every  residuals of linear lines at a specific angle 
	4. angle with least sum of the squared residuals is the Line to be fitted 
least squared method

	- [Regression and R-Squared (2.2) (youtube.com)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-TtIPF0fCU)
## Measure How Well it Fits the Data.

 - SS(mean) ,(Sum of Squared around the mean ) = $(data - mean)^2$
 - var(mean) ,(Variation around the mean)= $\frac{SS(mean)}{n}$
	 - $n$ = no. of observation 
- var(fit) ,(Variation around the fitted line )= $\frac{SS(mean)}{n}$
