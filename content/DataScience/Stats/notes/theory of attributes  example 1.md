### Example:

**Scenario:** Suppose you have a standard deck of 52 cards. You draw one card, replace it, shuffle the deck, and then draw a second card. Event AAA is "drawing a King," and event BBB is "drawing a Heart."

### Calculations:

1. **Probability of A:** There are 4 Kings in the deck, so $P(A) = \frac{4}{52} = \frac{1}{13}​$.
    
2. **Probability of B:** There are 13 Hearts in the deck, so $P(B) = \frac{13}{52} = \frac{1}{4}$​.
    
3. **Are A and B independent?** Yes, because drawing a King does not affect the probability of drawing a Heart when the card is replaced.
    
4. **Probability of A∩B:** The intersection $A \cap B$ represents drawing a King that is also a Heart (the King of Hearts). Since there is 1 such card, $P(A \cap B) = \frac{1}{52}$​.
    
5. **Verify Independence Formula:**
    
    $P(B) = \frac{1}{13} \cdot \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{52}$
    
    This matches P(A∩B)P(A \cap B)P(A∩B), confirming the independence
