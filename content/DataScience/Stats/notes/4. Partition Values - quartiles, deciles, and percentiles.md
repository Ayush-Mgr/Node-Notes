- **Quartiles**:
	- Formula: ‚Äã
		$Q_k = \frac{k(n+1)}{4}$
	- $k$ is the quartile number (1 for $Q_1$‚Äã, 2 for $Q_2$‚Äã, 3 for $Q_3$‚Äã),
	- $n$ is the number of data points in the series.
	    - Divide a series into 4 equal parts.
	    - There are three quartiles: Q1, Q2, and Q3.
	    - Q1 (lower quartile) covers 25% of the data.
	    - Q2 is the median (middle value), covering 50% of the data.
	    - Q3 (upper quartile) covers 75% of the data.
- **Deciles**:
	- formula :
		- $D_k = \frac{k(n+1)}{10}$
    - Divide a series into 10 equal parts.
    - There are nine deciles: D1, D2, ..., D9.
    - These represent points like the first decile, second decile, and so on.
- **Percentiles**:
	- Formula:
		    $P_k = \frac{k(n+1)}{100}$
    - Divide a series into 100 equal parts.
    - There are 99 percentiles: P1, P2, ..., P99.
    - These represent divisions for every 1% of the data set.
