Great! Let’s go over the **standard regression form**:

### 🔹 Standard Simple Linear Regression Equation

$$\hat{y}=b_0+b_1x$$

Where:

- $\hat{y}$ = predicted value of $y$
    
- $b_0$ = $y$ intercept (value of $y$ when $x = 0$)
    
- $b_1$ = slope (change in $y$ for each unit change in $x$)
    
    - $b_1=\frac{∑(xi−\bar{x})(yi−\bar{y})}{∑(xi−\bar{x})2}$
        
- $x$ = independent variable
    

---

## ✅ Example Problem

### 🎯 Problem:

A teacher collects data from 5 students showing how many hours they studied ($x$) and the marks they scored ($y$):

|Hours Studied ($x$)|Marks Scored ($y$)|
|---|---|
|1|50|
|2|55|
|3|65|
|4|70|
|5|75|

Find the **linear regression equation** $\hat{y} = b_0 + b_1x$ and use it to **predict the marks** for a student who studies **6 hours**.

---

## 🔍 Solution

### Step 1: Find means

$$\bar{y}=\frac{1+2+3+4+5}{5}=3$$
$$\bar{y}=\frac{50+55+65+70+75}{5}=63$$

---

### Step 2: Find slope $b_1$

Formula:

$$b_1=\frac{∑(xi−\bar{x})(yi−\bar{y})}{∑(xi−\bar{x})2}$$

| $x$ | $y$ | $x - \bar{x}$ | $y - \bar{y}$ | $(x - \bar{x})(y - \bar{y})$ | $(x - \bar{x})^2$ |     |
| --- | --- | ------------- | ------------- | ---------------------------- | ----------------- | --- |
| 1   | 50  | -2            | -13           | 26                           | 4                 |     |
| 2   | 55  | -1            | -8            | 8                            | 1                 |     |
| 3   | 65  | 0             | 2             | 0                            | 0                 |     |
| 4   | 70  | 1             | 7             | 7                            | 1                 |     |
| 5   | 75  | 2             | 12            | 24                           | 4                 |     |

Now:

$$

b_1 = \frac{26 + 8 + 0 + 7 + 24}{4 + 1 + 0 + 1 + 4} = \frac{65}{10} = 6.5

$$

---

### Step 3: Find intercept $b_0$

$b_0=\bar{y}−b_1\bar{x}$
$=63−6.5⋅3$
$=63−19.5$
$=43.5$

---

### ✅ Final Regression Equation:

$$\hat{y}=43.5+6.5x$$

---

### 🎯 Prediction for 6 hours of study:

$\hat{y}=43.5+6.5⋅6=43.5+39=82.5$

---

### ✅ Answer:

If a student studies for **6 hours**, the model predicts they will score **82.5 marks**.
