### **Interpreting Skewness**

- **Positive Skewness**:
    
    - If the coefficient is **greater than 0** (e.g., $\text{Skewness} > 0$), it indicates that the distribution has a longer tail on the right side, meaning the [[Mean]] is greater than the [[Median]]. This is often observed in datasets where there are a few exceptionally high values.
- **Negative Skewness**:
    
    - If the coefficient is **less than 0** (e.g., $\text{Skewness} < 0$), it indicates that the distribution has a longer tail on the left side, meaning the [[Mean]] is less than the [[Median]]. This is typical in datasets with a few exceptionally low values.
- **Zero Skewness**:
    
    - If the coefficient is **equal to 0** (e.g., $\text{Skewness} = 0$), it suggests that the distribution is symmetric, with the [[Mean]] and median being approximately equal
