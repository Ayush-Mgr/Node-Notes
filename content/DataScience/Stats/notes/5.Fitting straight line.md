	  - Compute $(a)$ (intercept):

## Transforming normal equations

To transform the expression $(\sum (y_i - a - b x_i)^2)$ into the form $( \sum y_i = a n + b \sum x_i )$, you need to derive the conditions for minimizing the sum of squared errors (SSE) in a linear regression problem. Here’s the breakdown:

### Expression:
$$
\text{SSE} = \sum (y_i - a - b x_i)^2
$$
Here:
- $(y_i)$ are the observed values.
- $(x_i)$ are the explanatory variables.
- $(a)$ and $(b)$ are parameters of the linear model $(y = a + bx)$.

### Step 1: Minimize SSE
To find $(a)$ and $(b)$, take the partial derivatives of the SSE with respect to $(a)$ and $(b)$ and set them to zero.

#### Partial derivative w.r.t. $(a)$:
$$
\frac{\partial}{\partial a} \sum (y_i - a - b x_i)^2 = -2 \sum (y_i - a - b x_i) = 0
$$
Rearrange:
$$
\sum y_i = a n + b \sum x_i
$$
This is the equation you're asking for.

#### Partial derivative w.r.t. $(b)$:
$$
\frac{\partial}{\partial b} \sum (y_i - a - b x_i)^2 = -2 \sum x_i (y_i - a - b x_i) = 0
$$
Rearrange:
$$
\sum x_i y_i = a \sum x_i + b \sum x_i^2
$$

### Interpretation:
The equations are:
1. $(\sum y_i = a n + b \sum x_i)$
2. $(\sum x_i y_i = a \sum x_i + b \sum x_i^2)$

These two equations form the normal equations for linear regression, which can be solved to find $(a)$ and $(b)$.

## EXAMLE 2 simple method 
Let’s work through a small dataset and compute the slope (($b$)) and intercept (($a$)) step-by-step using the method of **simultaneous subtraction** of the normal equations.

---

### Example Dataset:

Suppose we have the following data points:
$$
(x, y) = \{(1, 2), (2, 3), (3, 5)\}.
$$

### Step 1: Compute the required sums
We need to compute the following sums to substitute into the normal equations:
- ($\sum x$),
- ($\sum y$),
- ($\sum x^2$),
- ($\sum xy$),
- ($n$)(the number of data points).

$$
\begin{aligned}
n &= 3, \\
\sum x &= 1 + 2 + 3 = 6, \\
\sum y &= 2 + 3 + 5 = 10, \\
\sum x^2 &= 1^2 + 2^2 + 3^2 = 1 + 4 + 9 = 14, \\
\sum xy &= (1)(2) + (2)(3) + (3)(5) = 2 + 6 + 15 = 23.
\end{aligned}
$$

---

### Step 2: Write the normal equations
The normal equations are:
1. ($\sum y = n a + b \sum x$):
   $$
   10 = 3a + 6b. \tag{1}
   $$

2. ($\sum xy = a \sum x + b \sum x^2$):
   $$
   23 = 6a + 14b. \tag{2}
   $$

---

### Step 3: Subtract the equations
We eliminate ($a$)by aligning and subtracting the equations. Multiply Equation (1) by ($2$)(to make ($6a$)in both equations) , making $a$ in both equation similar so it cancels out to get $b$:

$$
2 \times (10 = 3a + 6b) \implies 20 = 6a + 12b. \tag{3}
$$

Now subtract Equation (3) from Equation (2):

$$
23 - 20 = (6a + 14b) - (6a + 12b).
$$

Simplify:
$$
3 = 2b.
$$

Solve for ($b$):
$$
b = \frac{3}{2} = 1.5.
$$

---

### Step 4: Solve for ($a$)
Substitute ($b = 1.5$)into Equation (1):
$$
10 = 3a + 6(1.5).
$$

Simplify:
$$
10 = 3a + 9.
$$

Solve for ($a$):
$$
a = \frac{10 - 9}{3} = \frac{1}{3}.
$$

---

### Final Result:
The regression line is:
$$
y = a + bx = \frac{1}{3} + 1.5x.
$$

---

Using the derived line ($y = \frac{1}{3} + 1.5x$), you can check how well it fits the data points by calculating residuals or comparing predictions against actual values.
