## 🧠 Introduction

This test checks whether **two independent population proportions** are significantly different from each other.

Use it when:
- You have **two categorical samples** (e.g., pass/fail, yes/no).
- You want to compare proportions $p_1$ and $p_2$ from two independent groups.

---

## ✅ Assumptions

- The samples are **independent**.
- Each sample follows a **binomial distribution**.
- The **normal approximation** is valid if:

$$
n_1 \hat{p}_1 \geq 5, \quad n_1(1 - \hat{p}_1) \geq 5 \\
n_2 \hat{p}_2 \geq 5, \quad n_2(1 - \hat{p}_2) \geq 5
$$

---

## 📐 Hypotheses

- **Null Hypothesis**: $H_0: p_1 = p_2$
- **Alternative Hypothesis** $H_1$:
  - $p_1 \neq p_2$ (two-tailed)
  - $p_1 > p_2$ or $p_1 < p_2$ (one-tailed)

---

## 🧮 Test Statistic

The Z-statistic is calculated using:

$$
Z = \frac{\hat{p}_1 - \hat{p}_2}
{\sqrt{ \hat{p}(1 - \hat{p}) \left( \frac{1}{n_1} + \frac{1}{n_2} \right) }}
$$

Where:
- $\hat{p}_1 = \frac{x_1}{n_1}$: sample proportion from group 1  
- $\hat{p}_2 = \frac{x_2}{n_2}$: sample proportion from group 2  
- $\hat{p} = \frac{x_1 + x_2}{n_1 + n_2}$: **pooled** proportion  
- $x_1, x_2$: number of successes in groups 1 and 2  
- $n_1, n_2$: sample sizes

---

## 📋 Example

> A university wants to compare pass rates of students from two colleges in a math exam.  
> College A: 60 passed out of 100.  
> College B: 70 passed out of 120.  
> Test for difference at 5% significance level.

### Step 1: Define Values

- $x_1 = 60$, $n_1 = 100$ → $\hat{p}_1 = 0.60$  
- $x_2 = 70$, $n_2 = 120$ → $\hat{p}_2 = 0.583$  
- Pooled proportion:

$$
\hat{p} = \frac{60 + 70}{100 + 120} = \frac{130}{220} \approx 0.591
$$

---

### Step 2: Compute Z

$$
Z = \frac{0.60 - 0.583}
{\sqrt{0.591 \cdot (1 - 0.591) \left( \frac{1}{100} + \frac{1}{120} \right)}}
= \frac{0.017}{\sqrt{0.241 \cdot (0.0183)}}
= \frac{0.017}{0.0662} \approx 0.257
$$

---

### Step 3: Conclusion

Compare with $Z_{\text{crit}} = \pm 1.96$ (two-tailed test, 5% level)  
Since $|Z| = 0.257 < 1.96$, **fail to reject $H_0$**.

✅ No significant difference in pass rates between the two colleges.
