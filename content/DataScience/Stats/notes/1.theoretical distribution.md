When we study something random (like flipping a coin or rolling a die), we sometimes create a **[[theoretical model]]** to predict the outcomes instead of doing the experiment repeatedly. 

- The **probability** of each outcome (like heads or tails) can be described using a **function**like [[4.Classic Probability|P(head)]] that depends on a [[random variable|random variable]] (which represents the outcomes, like "1" for heads and "0" for tails). 
- This kind of model that predicts probabilities based on the random variable is called a **==theoretical distribution==**.

For example, if we flip a fair coin, the theoretical distribution says the probability of heads is 0.5, and tails is 0.5. This is a theoretical prediction, not based on actual flips but on logic and assumptions.
