Hypothesis: A **hypothesis** is a **testable statement or prediction** about the relationship between two or more variables
### ✅ What Is the "Not Null" Hypothesis?

In statistics, the **alternative hypothesis** is the logical opposite of the **null hypothesis**. If the null says "**nothing's different**," the alternative says "**something is different**."

---

### example

#### Drug A vs. Drug B

- **Null Hypothesis (H₀)**: There’s **no difference** in recovery times.
    
- **Alternative Hypothesis (H₁)**: Drug A leads to a **real difference** in recovery times — perhaps people recover **15 hours faster**.
    

So when scientists collect data:

- If there's **strong evidence**, they **reject H₀** and lean toward **H₁**.
    
- If the evidence is weak, they **fail to reject H₀** — meaning they _can’t_ support H₁ confidently.
    

---

###  (to Simplify Further)

Imagine you're checking if:

- **Pizza A tastes better than Pizza B.**
    
- **H₀**: There’s no real difference — people are picking randomly.
    
- **H₁**: Pizza A **is genuinely** better.
    

When 100 people taste and 85 pick Pizza A, that’s **unlikely to happen by chance**, so you may **reject H₀** and say Pizza A is probably better → **H₁ gets support**.

---

### 🔑 Key Point

|Term|Meaning|
|---|---|
|**Null Hypothesis (H₀)**|No difference. Any result is due to chance.|
|**Alternative Hypothesis (H₁)**|There **is** a difference. Something real is happening.|
|**Reject H₀**|There’s strong evidence supporting H₁.|
|**Fail to reject H₀**|Not enough evidence. H₁ remains unproven.|

You can briefly add this clarification in **Section 4 or 5**, for example:
