Bayes' Theorem highlights the importance of prior probabilities and how they affect decision-making when new evidence is introduced.
- For example, if the risk of developing health problems is known to increase with age, Bayes' theorem allows the risk to someone of a known age to be assessed more accurately by conditioning it relative to their age, rather than assuming that the person is typical of the population as a whole

### Formula:
$$
P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)}
$$

### Terms:
1. **$P(A|B)$:** The posterior probability. The probability of event $A$ (the hypothesis) given event $B$ (the evidence).
2. **$P(B|A)$:** The likelihood. The probability of event $B$ given that $A$ is true.
3. **$P(A)$:** The prior probability. The initial probability of $A$ before considering $B$.
4. **$P(B)$:** The marginal probability. The overall probability of $B$ occurring.

---
## example 
_In a city, 60% of the vehicles are cars and 40% are motorcycles. The probability of a car being involved in an accident is 10%, while the probability of a motorcycle being involved in an accident is 5%. If an accident occurred, what is the probability that it involved a car?_


---

### Problem Breakdown:
- **Proportion of vehicles**:
  - $P(Car) = 0.6$
  - $P(Motorcycle) = 0.4$

- **Probability of being in an accident**:
  - $P(Accident | Car) = 0.1$
  - $P(Accident | Motorcycle) = 0.05$

- We want to find $P(Car | Accident)$, the probability that an accident involved a car.

---

### Step 1: Total Probability of an Accident
The [[5.theorem of probability#^265b05|total probability]] of an accident, $P(Accident)$, can be calculated using the law of total probability:
$$
P(Accident) = P(Accident | Car) \cdot P(Car) + P(Accident | Motorcycle) \cdot P(Motorcycle)
$$

Substitute the values:
$$
P(Accident) = (0.1 \cdot 0.6) + (0.05 \cdot 0.4)
$$
$$
P(Accident) = 0.06 + 0.02 = 0.08
$$

---

### Step 2: Apply Bayes' Theorem
Bayes' Theorem states:
$$
P(Car | Accident) = \frac{P(Accident | Car) \cdot P(Car)}{P(Accident)}
$$

Substitute the values:
$$
P(Car | Accident) = \frac{0.1 \cdot 0.6}{0.08}
$$
$$
P(Car | Accident) = \frac{0.06}{0.08} = 0.75
$$

---

### Final Answer:
The probability that an accident involved a car is **75%**.
