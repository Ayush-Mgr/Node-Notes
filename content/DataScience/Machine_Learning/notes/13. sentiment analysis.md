### 1. What is Sentiment Analysis?

**Sentiment Analysis** (also called Opinion Mining) is the Natural Language Processing (NLP) task of detecting and interpreting the emotional tone behind a piece of text.

- **Goal:** Determine whether the expressed opinion is **Positive**, **Negative**, or **Neutral**.
    

### 2. What is Opinion Mining?

**Opinion Mining** is the computational study of people's opinions, sentiments, attitudes, and emotions expressed in text.

- **Focus:** It is a subtask that specifically identifies **who** expressed **what opinion** about **which target** or feature.
    

#### Example Comparison

|Sentence|Sentiment Analysis|Opinion Mining|
|---|---|---|
|_"I love the camera quality of this phone, but the battery is terrible."_|**Mixed** (Positive + Negative)|**Opinion 1:** Camera $\to$Positive<br><br>**Opinion 2:** Battery $\to$Negative|

### 3. Levels of Analysis

|Level|Description|Example|
|---|---|---|
|**Document-level**|The entire document carries one aggregate opinion.|A full review saying _"This movie is amazing!"_ $\to$**Positive**|
|**Sentence-level**|Each sentence is analyzed separately.|_"The screen is good. The battery drains fast."_|
|**Aspect-level**|Opinion on specific features (targets).|**Aspect 1:** Screen $\to$ Positive<br><br>**Aspect 2:** Battery $\to$ Negative|
|**Entity-level**|Different entities evaluated in one text.|_"Apple is great, but I prefer Samsung for displays."_|

### 4. Techniques in Sentiment Analysis

#### 1. Lexicon-Based Approaches

- Uses a predefined list of sentiment words with polarity scores.
    
- **Pros:** Simple and interpretable.
    
- **Example:** "amazing" (+1.0), "horrible" (-1.0).
    

#### 2. Machine Learning Approaches

- Requires labeled training data.
    
- **Models:** Logistic Regression, Naive Bayes, SVM.
    
- **Features:** TF-IDF, Bag of Words, n-grams.
    

#### 3. Deep Learning Approaches

- Uses neural networks to handle context, sarcasm, and negation.
    
- **Models:** LSTM, BERT, RoBERTa.
    
- **Input:** Word embeddings or pre-trained language models.
    

### 5. Detailed Example: Aspect-Based Opinion Extraction

**Sentence:** _"The camera is awesome, but the battery life is too short."_

|Aspect|Opinion Phrase|Polarity|
|---|---|---|
|**camera**|is awesome|**Positive**|
|**battery life**|is too short|**Negative**|

### 6. Comparison: Sentiment Analysis vs. Opinion Mining

|Feature|Sentiment Analysis|Opinion Mining|
|---|---|---|
|**Goal**|Detect emotion in text.|Extract opinions on specific aspects/entities.|
|**Granularity**|Document / Sentence level.|Aspect / Entity level.|
|**Output**|Positive/Negative/Neutral label.|Aspect-opinion pairs with sentiment polarity.|
|**Application**|Social media monitoring, general reviews.|Product feedback, detailed surveys.|

### 7. Real-World Use Cases

|Domain|Use Case|
|---|---|
|**E-commerce**|Understand customer opinions on product features (e.g., camera vs. battery).|
|**Politics**|Track sentiment toward political parties or leaders.|
|**Finance**|Analyze market sentiment from news headlines.|
|**Education**|Analyze student feedback on courses and faculty.|
|**Healthcare**|Analyze patient feedback on doctors, services, or medications.|

### 8. Challenges

1. **Sarcasm & Irony**
    
    - _Example:_ "I just love how my phone dies in 2 hours." $\to$ Actually **Negative**.
        
2. **Negation**
    
    - _Example:_ "The movie wasn't bad." $\to$ **Positive** meaning.
        
3. **Context Dependence**
    
    - _Example:_ "It's hot." $\to$ Good for food, bad for weather.
        
4. **Ambiguity**
    
    - _Example:_ "The interface is simple." $\to$ Could be positive (easy to use) or negative (lacking features).
        
5. **Domain Adaptation**
    
    - Words may have different polarities across domains (e.g., "unpredictable" is good for a movie plot, bad for a car's steering).
