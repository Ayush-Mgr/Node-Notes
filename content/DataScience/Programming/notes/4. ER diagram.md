-  Entity relationship diagram  is a framework / Graphical representation that is created using specialized symbol of our data frame or how the data frame is gonna be.
- It shows the relationship between data  inside the data frame 
 - it is based on the three main attributes: entity, relationship, and attributes

the reason  we need this ER diagram is for a better understanding of technical data, designers and other for example,  
A school wants a database of their students and they hire a data designers and designers do not input the data directly first they will create an ER diagram with the help of school officials and then only then proceed to work on the database because ER diagram is far easier to understand the framework of how data is gonna be

Components of ER diagram:

Entity: could be living or non-living component, but necessary  to have have a [[primary key]]
- weak entity: it is an entity that relies on other entity, **Does not have a primary key** of its own
	- whether the entity is weak or not boils down to this
		- **Does it have a globally unique primary key by design?**
		- Or does it **depend on another entity’s key** to be uniquely identified?



 Attribute: it describes the attributes or the characteristics of entity
- key: it is an attribute which uniquely identifies the entity from the entire group
- composite: a composite attribute is an attribute's combination example  attribute name is composed of three additional attributes which is first name + middle name +   last name
- multi-value: and it should be that good have multiple values example attribute  phone number but it could store two additional phone numbers
- derived: calculate it from another attribute example attribute date of birth equals two current date minus age.

Relationship: the kind of relationship, the entity one and the entity two has.
- one to one relationship : customer ID and customer
- one to many relationship: customer and customer orders
- many to one relationship: customer orders and customer
- many to many relationship: customer and product
- Derived attributes in an Entity-Relationship (ER) model are ==properties whose values aren't stored directly but are **calculated from other attributes**==
