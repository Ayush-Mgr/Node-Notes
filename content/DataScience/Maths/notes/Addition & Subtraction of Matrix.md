### **Matrix Addition & Subtraction Example**  

#### **Matrix Addition**  
Two matrices can be added only if they have the **same dimensions** (same number of rows and columns). Addition is performed **element-wise** by adding corresponding elements.  

#### **Example**  
Let’s add two $2 \times 2$ matrices:  

$$
A =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 \\
3 & 4
\end{bmatrix},
\quad
B =
\begin{bmatrix}
5 & 6 \\
7 & 8
\end{bmatrix}
$$

**Step-by-step addition:**  
$$
A + B =
\begin{bmatrix}
1+5 & 2+6 \\
3+7 & 4+8
\end{bmatrix}
$$

$$
=
\begin{bmatrix}
6 & 8 \\
10 & 12
\end{bmatrix}
$$

---

#### **Matrix Subtraction**  
Subtraction is also **element-wise**, where corresponding elements of the second matrix are subtracted from the first.

#### **Example**  
Using the same matrices:

$$
A -
B =
\begin{bmatrix}
1-5 & 2-6 \\
3-7 & 4-8
\end{bmatrix}
$$

$$
=
\begin{bmatrix}
-4 & -4 \\
-4 & -4
\end{bmatrix}
$$

---

### **Key Points:**
✅ Matrices must have the same dimensions.  
✅ Addition and subtraction are done element-wise.  
✅ The result is another matrix of the same dimensions.
