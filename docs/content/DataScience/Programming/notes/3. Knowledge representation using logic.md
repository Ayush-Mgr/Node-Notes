### **1. [[1 . Propositional logic|Propositional Logic]]**
- **Propositional Logic** (also known as **Sentential Logic**) is used to represent knowledge using **propositions** that can either be **true** or **false**.
- The knowledge is represented using variables and logical connectives like **AND (∧)**, **OR (∨)**, **NOT (¬)**, **IMPLIES (→)**, and **IF AND ONLY IF (↔)**.
  
#### Example:
- Let **P** = "It is raining."
- Let **Q** = "The ground is wet."
  
The knowledge could be:
$$
P \rightarrow Q \quad \text{(If it rains, then the ground is wet)}
$$

---

### **2. [[2.First-order logic and predicate calculus|Predicate Logic (First-Order Logic)]]**
- **Predicate Logic** (or **First-Order Logic**) is a more powerful form of logic that allows us to represent relationships and properties using **predicates**, **quantifiers**, and **variables**.
- It goes beyond propositional logic by representing **objects** and their **relationships**.

#### Example:
- Let **Human(x)** represent "x is a human."
- Let **Mortal(x)** represent "x is mortal."
  
We can express:
$$
\forall x (Human(x) \rightarrow Mortal(x)) \quad \text{(All humans are mortal)}
$$

---

### **3. Rules → If-Then**
- **Rules** are a way of representing knowledge in the form of **condition-action** statements: **IF (condition) THEN (action)**. ^4a88a4
- These are commonly used in expert systems and reasoning processes.

#### Example:
- **Rule 1**: "If a person is a student, then they are under 25 years old."
  $$
  \forall x (Student(x) \rightarrow Age(x) < 25)
  $$
- **Rule 2**: "If it is raining, then carry an umbrella."
  $$
  Rain(x) \rightarrow CarryUmbrella(x)
  $$

---

### **4. Semantic Networks (Google Graph, Meaning Graph)**
- **Semantic Networks** represent concepts and their relationships as a **graph**, where **nodes** represent concepts, and **edges** represent relationships between them. ^a7e3fa
- They are widely used for storing knowledge in AI systems, like **Google Knowledge Graph**.
  
#### Example:
- the concept mammal which has characteristics of four legs ahead and a tail and further inside mammal, we could find cows, tigers and some exceptions like humans without tales , their inter connection and relation with each other defines semantic networks

---

### **5. Frames → Slots and Fillers**
- **Frames** are a type of knowledge representation where information is stored in **slots** (attributes or properties) and **fillers** (values or data). ^b18a94
- Frames are similar to object-oriented programming's **classes** and **objects**.

#### Example:
- A frame for representing a **"Car"** might have the following slots:
  - **Make** (filler: "Toyota")
  - **Model** (filler: "Camry")
  - **Year** (filler: "2020")
  - **Color** (filler: "Red")

---

### **6. Objects and Attributes**
- In **Object-Oriented Knowledge Representation**, **objects** represent entities or things, and **attributes** describe their properties.
  
#### Example:
- **Object**: "Car"
  - **Attributes**: "Color", "Make", "Model", "Year"
  
An object can also represent a real-world entity with specific properties, and these properties can be used to reason about the object.

---

### **7. Scripts**
- **Scripts** are a type of knowledge representation that describes **events** and the typical sequence of actions or events that occur in a particular situation.
- They help in representing knowledge of **stereotypical sequences** (e.g., a typical visit to a restaurant or a doctor's office).

#### Example:
- A script for a **restaurant visit** might include:
  1. **Entering the restaurant**.
  2. **Being seated**.
  3. **Ordering food**.
  4. **Eating the food**.
  5. **Paying the bill**.


---

## **Summary**


- **Propositional Logic** is simple but limited to representing basic facts.
- **Predicate Logic** adds depth by allowing for relationships, objects, and quantification.
- **Rules (If-Then)** are used in expert systems to encode condition-action knowledge.
- **Semantic Networks** represent relationships between concepts and are used in large knowledge graphs (e.g., Google Knowledge Graph).
- **Frames** are similar to object-oriented programming and represent structured entities with attributes.
- **Objects and Attributes** allow for representing real-world entities and their properties.
- **Scripts** represent stereotypical sequences of events, useful for reasoning about common scenarios. ^42339a
