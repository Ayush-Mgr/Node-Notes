#  State space representation
 
State-space representation in problem-solving and artificial intelligence defines a formal framework to model and solve problems. 
-  $$S = \{S, A, action(S), result(s, a), cost(s, a)\}$$
  - **$S$**: Represents the set of all possible states, including the start state and goal states.
  - **$A$**: Denotes the set of actions or rules applicable to the states.
  - **$action(S)$**: Specifies the actions available for a given state $S$.
  - **$result(s, a)$**: Indicates the state resulting from applying action $a$ in state $s$.
  - **$cost(s, a)$**: Represents the cost associated with performing action $a$ in state $s$.

## Example
Let’s consider an example of a **state-space representation** for the classic **8-puzzle problem**:

---

### Problem:
The **8-puzzle** consists of a 3x3 grid with 8 numbered tiles (1-8) and one empty space. The objective is to move the tiles to arrange them in a goal state.

### State-Space Representation:
1. **Set of States ($S$)**:
   - Each state represents a unique configuration of the grid.
   - For example:
     $$
     \text{Start State: } 
     \begin{bmatrix}
     1 & 2 & 3 \\
     4 & 5 & 6 \\
     7 & 8 & 0 
     \end{bmatrix}, 
     \text{Goal State: } 
     \begin{bmatrix}
     1 & 2 & 3 \\
     4 & 5 & 6 \\
     7 & 8 & 0 
     \end{bmatrix}
     $$

2. **Actions ($A$)**:
   - Possible moves for the empty space (0): **Up, Down, Left, Right**.
   - Not all moves are valid in every state (e.g., "Up" isn't valid if the empty space is in the top row).

3. **Action Function ($action(S)$)**:
   - Determines the set of valid moves for the empty space based on its position in $S$.
   - Example: If $S$ is:
     $$
     \begin{bmatrix}
     1 & 2 & 3 \\
     4 & 5 & 6 \\
     7 & 8 & 0 
     \end{bmatrix}
     $$
     Valid actions are: **Up**, **Left**.

4. **Result Function ($result(s, a)$)**:
   - Describes the resulting state after applying an action $a$ in state $s$.
   - Example: Applying **"Up"** to move the empty space (0) up in the start state yields:
     $$
     \begin{bmatrix}
     1 & 2 & 3 \\
     4 & 5 & 0 \\
     7 & 8 & 6 
     \end{bmatrix}
     $$

5. **Cost Function ($cost(s, a)$)**:
   - Represents the cost of performing an action $a$ in state $s$. For simplicity, assume each move has a cost of 1.

---

### Solving the Problem:
*Search algorithms like A* can use this representation:
1. **Start State**: Initial configuration.
2. **Goal Test**: Check if the current state matches the goal state.
3. **Path Cost**: Total cost of moves taken to reach a state.

Example Path for $S$ = Start State to Goal State:
1. Start:  
   $$
   \begin{bmatrix}
   1 & 2 & 3 \\
   4 & 5 & 6 \\
   7 & 8 & 0 
   \end{bmatrix}
   $$
2. Apply "Up":  
   $$
   \begin{bmatrix}
   1 & 2 & 3 \\
   4 & 5 & 0 \\
   7 & 8 & 6 
   \end{bmatrix}
   $$
3. Apply "Left":  
   $$
   \begin{bmatrix}
   1 & 2 & 3 \\
   4 & 0 & 5 \\
   7 & 8 & 6 
   \end{bmatrix}
   $$

This process continues until the goal state is reached!
