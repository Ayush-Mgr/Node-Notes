## 🧠 Introduction

This test evaluates whether a **single [[Population Proportions|population proportion]]** differs significantly from a **hypothesized value**.

Use it when:
- The variable is **categorical** (e.g., success/failure).
- You have **one sample** and want to test a claim about a population proportion $p$.

---

## ✅ Assumptions

- The sample follows a **binomial distribution**.
- Normal approximation is valid if:
  
$$
np_0 \geq 5 \quad \text{and} \quad n(1 - p_0) \geq 5
$$

---

## 📐 Hypotheses

- **Null Hypothesis**: $H_0: p = p_0$
- **Alternative Hypothesis** $H_1$:
  - $p \neq p_0$ (two-tailed)
  - $p > p_0$ (right-tailed)
  - $p < p_0$ (left-tailed)

---

## 🧮 Test Statistic

The Z-statistic is calculated using:

$$
Z = \frac{\hat{p} - p_0}{\sqrt{\frac{p_0 (1 - p_0)}{n}}}
$$

Where:
- $\hat{p} = \frac{x}{n}$: sample proportion
- $p_0$: hypothesized proportion
- $n$: sample size
- $x$: number of successes

---

## 📋 Example

> A smartphone company claims that 40% of customers prefer their brand.  
> A survey of 200 people shows that 90 prefer it.  
> Test this claim at a 5% significance level.

### Step 1: Define Parameters

- $p_0 = 0.4$
- $n = 200$, $x = 90$
- $\hat{p} = \frac{90}{200} = 0.45$

### Step 2: Calculate Z

$$
Z = \frac{0.45 - 0.4}{\sqrt{\frac{0.4 \cdot 0.6}{200}}}
= \frac{0.05}{\sqrt{0.0012}}
= \frac{0.05}{0.0346} \approx 1.45
$$

### Step 3: Conclusion

Compare with critical value $Z_{\text{crit}} = \pm 1.96$ (for two-tailed test at $\alpha = 0.05$)  
Since $|Z| = 1.45 < 1.96$, **fail to reject $H_0$**.

✅ The sample does **not provide enough evidence** to reject the company's claim.
