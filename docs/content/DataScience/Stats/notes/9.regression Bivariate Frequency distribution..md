- The regression [[2.Bivariate data#^230282|Bivariate Frequency distribution]]. Shows relationship between the _dependent mean value_ of $y$ and the corresponding _independent value_ of $X$ or the class marks.
- if we found linear curve in the data, we should say it is linear regression of $x$ and $y$

### EXAMPLE

- given data 

| (y $\downarrow$)\(x$\rightarrow$) | 0   | 1   | 2   | 3   | 4   |
| --------------------------------- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0                                 | -   | -   | -   | 4   | 3   |
| 1                                 | -   | -   | 18  | 36  | 9   |
| 2                                 | -   | 12  | 54  | 36  | 3   |
| 3                                 | 1   | 12  | 18  | 4   | -   |

---

| x   | Total of y-array                      | Marginal Frequency | Mean of y-array |
| --- | ------------------------------------- | ------------------ | --------------- |
| 0   | 1 x 3 = 3                             | 1                  | 3/1 = 3         |
| 1   | 12 x 2 + 12 x 3 = 60                  | 24                 | 60/24 = 2.5     |
| 2   | 18 x 1 + 54 x 2 + 18 x 3 = 180        | 90                 | 180/90 = 2      |
| 3   | 4 x 0 + 36 x 1 + 36 x 2 + 4 x 3 = 120 | 80                 | 120/80 = 1.5    |
| 4   | 3 x 0 + 9 x 1 + 3 x 2 = 15            | 15                 | 15/15 = 1       |
- here
	- [[2.Bivariate data#^98ac33|_marginal frequency_]] is the sum of data in a row
	-  mean of y-array = $\frac{\text{total of array}} {\text{ marginal frequency}}$
- from here we get increase $x$ value decreases the mean value of $y$ array by $–0.5$ so they are    co-linear which means if a plot $x$ and mean of $y$ , we get a straight line which is the regression of $y$ on $x$
- the [[7.2 Mean centered Regression#^eb0056|regression coefficient]] is change in the value of why when X is increment so we found $b_{xy}=  -0.05$
