### **Binomial Distribution**

The **binomial distribution** is a [[Probability distribution|probability distribution]] that models the number of successes in a fixed number of independent [[Bernoulli trials]], each with the same probability of success.

#### **Characteristics**
1. **Fixed Number of Trials** ($n$): 
   - The number of experiments or attempts is predetermined.
2. **Independent Trials**:
   - The outcome of one trial does not affect the outcome of others.
3. **Two Possible Outcomes**:
   - Each trial results in either **success** or **failure**.
4. **Constant Probability of Success** ($p$):
   - The probability of success remains the same for all trials.

#### **Definition**
If $X$ is the number of successes in $n$ independent trials, then $X$ follows a binomial distribution, denoted as:
$$
X \sim \text{Binomial}(n, p)
$$

The [[Probability Mass Function (PMF)|probability mass function]] (PMF) for $X$ is given by:
$$
P(X = x) = \binom{n}{x} p^x (1-p)^{n-x}
$$
where:
- $\binom{n}{x} = \frac{n!}{x!(n-x)!}$:  ([[Permutation and combinations|combinations]]).
- $x$: Number of successes ($x = 0, 1, 2, \dots, n$).
- $n:$ total number 
- $p$: Probability of success in a single trial.
- $1-p$: Probability of failure in a single trial.

#### **Example**
1. **Coin Toss**:
   - Toss a fair coin $n = 10$ times, with the probability of heads $p = 0.5$.
   - Let $X$ represent the number of heads.
   - The PMF is:
     $$
     P(X = x) = \binom{10}{x} (0.5)^x (0.5)^{10-x}
     $$
   - $X$ is the number of heads in 10 tosses.

2. **Defective Products**:
   - A factory produces $n = 20$ items, and the probability of an item being defective is $p = 0.1$.
   - Let $X$ represent the number of defective items.
   - The PMF is:
     $$
     P(X = x) = \binom{20}{x} (0.1)^x (0.9)^{20-x}
     $$
   - $X$ is the number of defective items out of 20.



The binomial distribution is foundational in probability and statistics, often used for modeling discrete events and hypothesis testing
