	- $P( X = \text{x or e} )$
- we signify random variable as $X(e)$
	-  $X(e):$ here hello is the function which assign real number to each simple point
	- $e:$ is the [[8.Sets And Probability#^70b6f1|sample point]] of [[8.Sets And Probability#^f4ff4c|sample space]] 

### Example: Toss of a Coin

Consider a random experiment where a coin is tossed:
- The sample space is $S = \{H, T\}$, where $H$ represents "Heads" and $T$ represents "Tails."
- Define a random variable $X$ such that:
  - $X(H) = 1$
  - $X(T) = 0$
- The probabilities are:
  - $P(X = 1) = P(H) = 0.5$
  - $P(X = 0) = P(T) = 0.5$

In this case, $X$ converts the outcomes $H$ and $T$ into numerical values $1$ and $0$, respectively.
