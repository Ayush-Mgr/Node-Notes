- It is a characteristic that indicates how much observation vary among themselves if observation is same dispersion will be zero more. More will be variations. More will be the dispersion.
- Measures of dispersion are of two types, absolute, and relative the absolute 

- **Absolute measures** calculate deviation in the units of what series has, so we cannot measure the deviation between different series with different units ^dd62a0
	- it includes 
		- [[Range & coefficient of range#^7ef133|Range]]
		- [[Standard_deviation & coefficient]]
		- [[variance  & its coefficient#^f36366|Variance]]
		- [[mean deviation]]

- **Relative measures** are pure numbers or percentage so different series with different units could be compared 
	- relative measure is the ratio of absolute major to its mean and it is expressed in percentage ^830a35
	- [[variance  & its coefficient#^2996cf|coefficient of variation]], [[qua|coefficient of quartile deviation]], and [[mean deviation|coefficient of main deviation ]]



**relation ship of S.D and other measures**
- Range = 6$\sigma$
- Q.D = 2/3$\sigma$
- M.D= 4/5$\sigma$
