Imagine you're playing a game of chance, like rolling a die. You don't know for sure what number you'll get, but you know the possible outcomes and how likely each one is. A probability distribution is a way of mathematically describing this uncertainty.

In other words, probability distribution is the group of properties of an events outcome, that helps you to get clear picture about the whole probabilities our outcomes

## **Example:**

Consider a fair six-sided die. Its probability distribution is:

- **Possible Values:** 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6
- **Respective Probabilities:** 1/6, 1/6, 1/6, 1/6, 1/6, 1/6

Knowing only the probability of rolling a 1 (1/6) doesn't give you a complete understanding of the die's behavior. You need the probabilities of all possible outcomes to fully grasp the distribution.
- it showed you that there isn't any unbiasedness in the dice and each in every outcomes have a equal probabilities

**In summary:** A probability distribution requires the probabilities of all possible outcomes to be complete, accurate, and useful for analysis and decision-making.
