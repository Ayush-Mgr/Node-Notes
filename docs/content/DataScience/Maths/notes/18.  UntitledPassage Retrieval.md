### 1. What is Passage Retrieval?

**Definition:** Passage retrieval finds relevant textual segments from a large document corpus that are most likely to contain the answer.

### 2. Real-world Scenarios

|Context|Description|
|---|---|
|**Google Search**|Extracts 2-3 sentence snippets from web pages.|
|**Medical QA**|Retrieves scientific abstracts.|
|**Legal QA**|Retrieves relevant court rulings or law excerpts.|
|**Enterprise Docs**|Retrieves user manuals, product descriptions.|

### 3. Techniques

#### A. Sparse Retrieval (Traditional IR)

- **Methods:** TF-IDF, BM25.
    
- **Mechanism:** Documents and queries are represented as **sparse vectors** (word counts).
    
- **Pros/Cons:** Fast and interpretable, but doesn't capture semantics well.
    

#### B. Dense Retrieval (Neural Embeddings)

- **Mechanism:** Embeddings for both question and passage using **BERT-based encoders**.
    
- **Matching:** Vector similarity (dot product or cosine).
    
- **Systems:** DPR, ColBERT, ANCE, GTR.
    

#### C. Hybrid Retrieval

- **Mechanism:** Combines sparse (BM25) + dense (DPR) methods.
    
- **Use Case:** Useful for noisy or domain-specific data where keywords matter but semantics are also needed.
    

### 4. Architecture: Dense Passage Retriever (DPR)

|Component|Description|
|---|---|
|**Encoder_Q**|Encodes the query into a vector.|
|**Encoder_P**|Encodes the passage into a vector.|
|**Loss**|Maximizes dot similarity between query and the _correct_ passage vs. _negatives_.|

**Example:**

- **Question:** _"Who discovered gravity?"_
    
- **Top Retrieved Passages:**
    
    1. _"Isaac Newton formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation..."_ (Selected)
        
    2. _"Galileo studied falling bodies, but not gravity per se..."_
