
**derivative** in mathematics measures how a function changes as its input changes. It's a fundamental concept in calculus and describes the **rate of change** of a quantity.
### What Is a Derivative?

- In simple terms, the derivative of a function $f(x)$ with respect to $x$ (written as $df/dx$​ or $f′(x)$) tells us the slope of the tangent line to the curve of the function at a particular point.
- It answers the question: _How fast is $y$ changing with respect to $x$?_
### Example:

For a simple function$f(x)=x^2$ :

- Its derivative is $xf′(x)=2x$.
- This tells us that the slope of the curve at any point $x$ is $2x$.
