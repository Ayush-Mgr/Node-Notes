A **subspace** is a subset of a larger vector space that follows all the [[1.1 vector Spaces|same rules]], making it a valid vector space in its own right.

### Subspaces in 2D and 3D

You are correct on all points:

- In a 2D vector space (`R²`), the most common non-trivial subspaces are **lines that pass through the origin**.
    
- In a 3D vector space (`R³`), your guess is spot on. The main examples are:
    
    - Any **line that passes through the origin** (a 1D subspace).
        
    - Any **plane that passes through the origin** (a 2D subspace).

### The Complete List (Proper and Trivial Subspaces)

Just to give you the full picture, for any given vector space, there are always two "trivial" subspaces: the space itself and the zero vector alone.

So, the complete list of all possible subspaces is:

- **For `R²`**:
    
    - The origin `{ (0,0) }` (0-dimensional)
        
    - Any line through the origin (1-dimensional)
        
    - All of `R²` itself (2-dimensional)

    - All of `R³` itself (3-dimensional)
